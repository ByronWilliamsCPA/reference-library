## Expanded Answers on High-Frequency Questions

### 3. *That* vs. *Which* (§5.254)

**Restrictive clause** (essential to identifying the noun): use *that*, no comma.

- "The statute **that** governs this transaction is ORS 174.010."

**Nonrestrictive clause** (adds information about an already-identified noun): use *which*,
with a comma before *which* (and after the clause if it doesn't end the sentence).

- "ORS 174.010, **which** was amended in 2023, governs this transaction."

**Quick test**: Remove the clause. If the sentence loses its essential meaning, the clause is
restrictive → *that*. If the sentence still identifies the right noun, the clause is
nonrestrictive → *which* + comma.

---

### 5. *None* — Singular or Plural (§5.258)

CMS uses notional agreement: match the verb to what *none* refers to.

- "None of the briefs **were** timely." (*None* = no briefs → plural concept → plural verb)
- "None of the money **was** recovered." (*None* = no amount → singular concept → singular verb)

EoS treated *none* as always singular ("None of them is guilty"). CMS overrides this: match
the notional meaning, not a fixed rule.

---

### 7. Em-Dash Alternatives

CMS permits em-dashes in several valid constructions (§6.85–6.87). The user-confirmed PCP
override bans them entirely. Use these substitutes:

| Em-dash use | Substitute | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parenthetical aside | Commas | "The motion, filed after the deadline, was denied." |
| Elaboration/amplification | Colon | "The result is clear: the motion must fail." |
| Insertion or digression | Parentheses | "The statute (enacted in 1981) was amended." |
| Abrupt break between clauses | Semicolon | "The record is undisputed; the motion fails." |

### 9. Quotation Marks and Punctuation (§6.9)

U.S. rule (CMS follows U.S. convention):

- **Periods and commas**: always inside the closing quotation mark.
- **Semicolons and colons**: always outside.
- **Question marks and exclamation points**: inside if part of the quoted matter; outside if not.

UK style (not used here) is the opposite for periods and commas. If reviewing a document from
a British source, expect to see punctuation outside the closing mark.

---

### 10. Possessive of Names Ending in *s* (§7.20)

**General rule**: Add *'s* to any singular name ending in *s*.

- *Williams's*, *Burns's*, *James's*, *Achilles's* (general names)

**Exception**: Classical and biblical names take only an apostrophe:

- *Jesus'*, *Moses'*, *Achilles'*, *Xerxes'*

**Plural names** (already plural): add only an apostrophe:

- "the Joneses' house," "the Williamses' firm"

---

### 17. Semicolon Before *However* (§6.57)

*However* (and other conjunctive adverbs: *therefore*, *moreover*, *consequently*,
*nevertheless*, *thus*, *hence*) connect two independent clauses but are not coordinating
conjunctions. They require a semicolon before them and a comma after.

- Correct: "The statute is clear; **however**, the court misapplied it."
- Incorrect: "The statute is clear, **however**, the court misapplied it." (comma splice)
- Incorrect: "The statute is clear. **However** the court misapplied it." (missing comma after)

---

### 19. Capitalization of *Court* and *State* in Legal Briefs

In Oregon legal writing, follow these conventions:

**Capitalize** *Court* when it refers to the specific court deciding the present case.

- "The **Court** must consider whether the statute is ambiguous."

**Lowercase** *court* for generic references or other courts.

- "The lower **court** applied the wrong standard."
- "Multiple **courts** have addressed this issue."

**Capitalize** *State* when Oregon is a party to the case.

- "The **State** argues that the conviction is supported by the evidence."

**Lowercase** *state* for generic or adjectival use.

- "**State** agencies are subject to the APA." (adjectival: not a proper name)
- "Other **states** have different rules."

## Errors Specific to AI-Generated Prose

These errors appear frequently in AI output. When reviewing AI-drafted text, check for:

| Pattern | Problem | Fix |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "comprised of" | Nonstandard | "The chapter **comprises** five sections." |
| "impact" as verb | Vague; prefer *affect* | "The amendment **affects** the definition." |
| "utilize" | Prefer *use* unless technical context | "**Use** this method." |
| "in order to" | Verbose | "**to** comply" |
| "due to the fact that" | Verbose | "**because**" |
| "prior to" | Verbose | "**before**" |
| "subsequent to" | Verbose | "**after**" |
| "at this point in time" | Verbose | "**now**" |
| "it is important to note that" | Filler | Delete; start the actual point |
| "it should be noted" | Passive filler | Delete or attribute to a source |
| Starting sentence with em-dash context | PCP ban | Rewrite using commas, colons, semicolons |

For a complete list of AI puffery terms and blacklisted phrases, see
`../../ai-detection.md`.
