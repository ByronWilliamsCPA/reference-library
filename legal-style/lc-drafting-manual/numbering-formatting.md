# Numbering and Formatting — Oregon Legislative Drafting

> **Source**: Oregon LC Bill Drafting Manual, 2024 Edition (19th Edition), Chapters 3, 8, 13,
> and 18
> **Scope**: Section numbering; subsection/paragraph hierarchy; ORS citations; internal
> cross-references; amendment formatting; string citations; Oregon Constitution references

---

## Quick Reference

| Element | Format |
|---------|--------|
| Bill section | SECTION 1. (all caps, period) |
| Subsection | (1), (2), (3)... |
| Paragraph | (a), (b), (c)... |
| Subparagraph | (A), (B), (C)... |
| Sub-subparagraph | (i), (ii), (iii)... |
| Skipped letter | "(L)" instead of "(l)" to avoid confusion with "(1)" |
| ORS citation | ORS 97.190 (no "§", no "section") |
| ORS range | ORS 97.190 to 97.320 (means to and including 97.320) |
| Internal bill reference | "section 4 of this (year) Act" |
| Oregon Constitution | "Article IV, section 20, Oregon Constitution" |
| Amendment: deleted text | In brackets and italicized — *[deleted text]* |
| Amendment: new text | Bolded — **new text** |

---

## 1. Bill Section Numbering

### Format

Every operative provision of a bill is a separate section. Sections are numbered sequentially
beginning with SECTION 1.

```
SECTION 1.
SECTION 2.
SECTION 3.
```

Rules:
- "SECTION" is in all capital letters
- Followed by the number and a period: "SECTION 1."
- Sections are numbered with Arabic numerals: 1, 2, 3 (not I, II, III)
- Section numbers are sequential throughout the bill — they do not restart in different parts
- Each section contains one substantive provision or grouping of related provisions

### What Goes in a Section

Each SECTION in a bill typically contains one of these:
- One amended ORS section (in full, with changes marked)
- One new statutory provision
- One special provision (effective date, repeal, emergency clause, etc.)

Do not combine two different ORS sections into the same bill SECTION.

---

## 2. Subdivision Hierarchy Within a Section

Oregon statutes use a five-level hierarchy within each section:

| Level | Designator | Example |
|-------|------------|---------|
| Section | SECTION 1. | SECTION 1. |
| Subsection | (1), (2)... | (1) A person shall... |
| Paragraph | (a), (b)... | (a) Submit an application; |
| Subparagraph | (A), (B)... | (A) In triplicate; |
| Sub-subparagraph | (i), (ii)... | (i) Signed by the applicant; |

### Using Subsections

Break a section into subsections when the section contains multiple distinct provisions or
when tabular arrangement is required. Label subsections (1), (2), (3) etc. in parentheses.

### Using Paragraphs

Paragraphs are the subdivisions within a subsection. They are labeled (a), (b), (c) etc.
in parentheses. Paragraphs are used for:
- Items in a tabular list
- Separate conditions within a subsection
- Separate alternatives within a subsection

### Subparagraphs

Subparagraphs are subdivisions within a paragraph. Labeled (A), (B), (C) etc.

### Sub-Subparagraphs

Sub-subparagraphs are subdivisions within a subparagraph. Labeled (i), (ii), (iii) etc.

### The "(L)" Exception

When paragraph designators reach the letter "l" (lowercase L), use "(L)" (uppercase L)
instead to avoid visual confusion with "(1)" (the numeral one).

The sequence is:
> (a), (b), (c), (d), (e), (f), (g), (h), (i), (j), (k), **(L)**, (m), (n)...

This exception applies only at the paragraph level where lowercase letters are used and
confusion with subsection numerals is possible. At the subparagraph level (uppercase letters),
"(L)" is just the normal uppercase L.

## 3. Cross-References Within a Bill

### Referring to a Subsection of the Same Section

> "as provided in **subsection (2) of this section**"

### Referring to a Paragraph

> "as provided in **paragraph (a) of subsection (1) of this section**"

### Referring to Another Section of the Same Bill (Before Codification)

During the bill draft stage (before the sections have been assigned ORS numbers), reference
other sections of the same bill using the bill's year:

> "as provided in **section 4 of this (year) Act**"

> "as used in **sections 1 to 7 of this (year) Act**"

Replace "(year)" with the actual four-digit year of the legislative session (e.g., "this
2025 Act").

### Referring to a Subsection of Another Section of the Same Bill

> "as provided in **subsection (3) of section 4 of this (year) Act**"

### Referring to an ORS Section (After Codification)

After the bill has been codified, cross-references to the same act's sections become ORS
citations. Legislative Counsel updates these during codification. The drafter uses "section
___ of this (year) Act" in the draft.

---

## 4. ORS Citations

### Format

ORS citations use the following format:

> ORS 97.190

Rules:
- "ORS" is not spelled out — it is always the abbreviation
- No "§" symbol
- No "section" before the number
- The chapter number precedes the decimal point; the section number follows
- Do not add "of the Oregon Revised Statutes" — "ORS" carries that meaning

### Multiple ORS Sections — Non-Consecutive

List non-consecutive sections separated by commas:

> ORS 97.190, 97.200 and 97.210

(No Oxford comma — "97.190, 97.200 and 97.210" not "97.190, 97.200, and 97.210")

### ORS Range — "To and Including"

When citing a range of ORS sections, use "to":

> ORS 97.190 to 97.320

The word "to" in an ORS range means "to and including" — it includes the last section named
(ORS 97.320) and all sections between the two endpoints. Do not write "to and including" —
"to" is sufficient.

### ORS Chapter

When referring to an entire chapter:

> ORS chapter 97

(lowercase "chapter")

### When to Use ORS Citations

Use ORS citations when:
- Referring to a provision in Oregon law outside the current bill
- Describing what law is being amended or repealed

Do not use ORS-style numbers for references within the current bill before codification —
use "section ___ of this (year) Act."

---

## 5. Oregon Constitution References

### Format

> Article IV, section 20, Oregon Constitution

Rules:
- "Article" is capitalized
- "section" is lowercase
- The name "Oregon Constitution" follows the citation (no "of the")
- No comma between "section 20" and "Oregon Constitution"

### Multiple Sections

> Article III, sections 2 and 3, Oregon Constitution

### Subsections of the Oregon Constitution

> Article IV, section 1 (4)(d), Oregon Constitution

(Use the same parenthetical designator system as ORS.)

---

## 6. United States Code Citations

When citing federal law:

> 42 U.S.C. 1983

or

> 42 U.S.C. section 1983

Both forms are acceptable. Use "U.S.C." not "U.S.C.A." (the annotated version).

---

## 7. Federal Register and Code of Federal Regulations

> 40 C.F.R. part 50

> 40 C.F.R. 50.6

"C.F.R." is the Code of Federal Regulations. "Federal Register" citations: "72 Fed. Reg.
26352 (May 9, 2007)."

## 8. Amendment Formatting — Bracket-and-Bold Convention

When a bill amends an existing ORS section, the entire amended section is set forth in the
bill showing all changes.

### Deleted Text

Text being removed from the existing statute is placed in brackets and italicized:

> *[deleted text]*

### New Text

Text being added to the statute is bolded:

> **new text**

### Unchanged Text

Text remaining unchanged appears in normal (light) face, without brackets or bold.

### Renumbered Subsections

When a subsection is renumbered (e.g., old (2) becomes (3) because a new subsection is
inserted), the old number appears in brackets and italicized, and the new number appears in
bold:

> *[(2)]* **(3)** A person shall...

### Complete Amendment Example

```
SECTION 1. ORS 609.010 is amended to read:

   609.010. As used in ORS 609.010 to 609.190:

   (1) "Animal" means a dog, cat [or other domestic animal] and livestock.

   (2) "Livestock" means cattle, horses, mules, donkeys, sheep and goats.

   [(2)] (3) "Owner" means the person having a property right in the animal.
```

In this example:
- "*[or other domestic animal]*" is deleted (brackets and italics)
- "**and livestock**" is added (bold)
- "**Livestock" means cattle, horses, mules, donkeys, sheep and goats.**" is an entirely new
  subsection (all bold)
- "*[(2)]*" shows the old number is deleted; "**(3)**" shows the new number

### Annotation Position

The ORS section number and heading appear before the amended text (e.g., "609.010.") and
are not bold — they are existing law.

---

## 9. Amendatory Language — Standard Phrases

### Amending an Existing Section

> SECTION ___. ORS [section number] is amended to read:

### Adding a Subsection to an Existing Section

> SECTION ___. ORS [section number] is amended by adding a new subsection to read:

### Amending a Subsection

> SECTION ___. ORS [section number] (subsection number) is amended to read:

(This is less common — usually the full section is set forth.)

### Creating New Sections for Codification

> SECTION ___. [Text of new section.]
>
> SECTION ___. Section ___ of this (year) Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter
> ___ .

### Repealing a Section

> SECTION ___. ORS [section number] is repealed.

---

## 10. Designation of New Sections for Codification

When a bill creates entirely new sections to be codified in the ORS, the codification
instruction specifies which ORS chapter they belong to:

**Single section:**

> SECTION ___. Section ___ of this (year) Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter
> ___ .

**Multiple sections:**

> SECTION ___. Sections ___ to ___ of this (year) Act are added to and made a part of ORS
> chapter ___ .

**Specified ORS section numbers (if pre-determined):**

> SECTION ___. Sections ___ to ___ of this (year) Act are added to and made a part of ORS
> chapter ___ and are designated as a series beginning with ORS ___ and ending with ORS
> ___ .

---

## 11. Section Headings and Captions

Section headings (called "captions" or "catchlines") appear in some ORS chapters to
summarize the content of each section. These are not part of the operative text of the
statute — they are editorial additions by Legislative Counsel during codification.

In a bill draft, section headings are generally not used. Do not add interpretive headings to
bill sections.

---

## 12. Enumerating Subsections When a Section Has Only One

When a section contains only one substantive provision and does not need to be divided, do
not assign it a subsection number — the section text runs directly without (1).

However, if a section has a definition subsection and then operative provisions, number them
(1), (2) etc.

> SECTION 4. A person who violates this (year) Act commits a Class A misdemeanor.

(No "(1)" needed — only one provision.)

Compare:

> SECTION 5. (1) As used in this section, "commercial fisher" means...
>
> (2) A commercial fisher shall obtain a license under this section.

(Two provisions — number them.)

---

## 13. Internal Numbering After Codification

During codification, Legislative Counsel:
- Assigns ORS section numbers to new sections
- Updates "section ___ of this (year) Act" cross-references to ORS citations
- Updates "As used in sections ___ to ___ of this (year) Act" scope clauses to "As used in
  ORS ___ to ___:"
- Renumbers subsections if needed for consistency

The drafter does not need to assign ORS numbers in the bill draft. Use bill section numbers
and "this (year) Act" references throughout.

---

## 14. Special Formatting for Certain Bill Elements

### Emergency Clause

The emergency clause is a separate SECTION at the end of the bill:

> SECTION ___. This (year) Act being necessary for the immediate preservation of the public
> peace, health and safety, an emergency is declared to exist, and this (year) Act takes
> effect on its passage.

### Effective Date Section

> SECTION ___. This (year) Act takes effect on [date].

### Repeal Section

> SECTION ___. ORS ___ is repealed.

or

> SECTION ___. ORS ___ to ___ are repealed.

### Codification Instruction Section

> SECTION ___. Section ___ of this (year) Act is added to and made a part of ORS chapter
> ___ .

---

## 15. Numbering in Joint Resolutions and Other Measures

Joint resolutions use a different numbering convention:
- Sections are numbered (1), (2), (3) in Arabic numerals with parentheses — not "SECTION 1."
- Subdivisions of a section are lettered (a), (b), (c) etc.

Concurrent resolutions and memorials follow similar conventions.

---

## 16. Amendments to Bills (Floor Amendments)

When an amendment is offered to a bill on the floor of the Legislature, the amendment itself
follows specific formatting:

### Amending a Section of a Bill

> Amend SB ___ by deleting everything after the enacting clause and inserting:
> [New bill text]

Or for targeted amendments:

> In SECTION 1, line 3, delete "[deleted text]" and insert "**new text**".

### When Bill Sections Are Renumbered by an Amendment

If a floor amendment inserts a new section into a bill, all subsequent section numbers must
be renumbered. The amendment should include a renumbering instruction:

> Renumber SECTIONS 3 through 7 as SECTIONS 4 through 8 and correct internal references.

---

## 17. Common Formatting Errors

- [ ] "Section" not capitalized in "SECTION 1." (should be all caps)
- [ ] Sections numbered with Roman numerals (use Arabic)
- [ ] Subsection "(l)" used instead of "(L)" (risks confusion with "(1)")
- [ ] ORS citation uses "§" symbol instead of "ORS"
- [ ] ORS citation writes "section 97.190" instead of "ORS 97.190"
- [ ] Range citation writes "ORS 97.190 through 97.320" instead of "ORS 97.190 to 97.320"
- [ ] Internal bill reference uses ORS-style numbering before codification
- [ ] Cross-references say "said section" or "the above section" instead of identifying by
     number
- [ ] Oregon Constitution citation missing "Oregon Constitution" at the end
- [ ] Amendment formatting uses "(deleted)" markup instead of brackets and italics
- [ ] Amendment formatting uses underline for new text instead of bold
- [ ] Oxford comma present in a list (ORS 97.190, 97.200, **and** 97.210 — remove the comma
     before "and")
- [ ] "Added to and made a part of" language omitted for new codified sections
- [ ] Two ORS sections amended in the same bill SECTION (each needs its own SECTION)
