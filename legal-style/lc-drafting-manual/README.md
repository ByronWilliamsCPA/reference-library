# Oregon Legislative Counsel Bill Drafting Manual — Reference Files

> **Source**: Oregon Legislative Counsel Bill Drafting Manual, 2024 Edition (19th Edition)
> **Published by**: Legislative Counsel Committee, State Capitol, Salem, Oregon 97301
> **Scope**: Oregon state legislative drafting standards and conventions
> **Purpose of these files**: LLM-usable reference for drafting Oregon bills and statutory text

---

## What Is This Manual?

The Oregon Legislative Counsel Bill Drafting Manual is the authoritative style guide for Oregon
state bill drafters. It governs the form, style, grammar, structure, and content of every bill
introduced in the Oregon Legislative Assembly. The Legislative Counsel's office applies these
rules when preparing bills at the request of legislators, legislative committees, agencies, and
members of the public.

These reference files distill the manual's rules and examples into four topical files designed
for rapid lookup. They are not a substitute for the full manual — complex drafting questions
should always be resolved by consulting the primary source.

### 3. `defined-terms.md`

Rules from Chapter 7 (Definitions; Act Names; Policy and Purpose Statements; Legislative
Findings) and relevant provisions of ORS chapter 174. Covers:

- When and where to define terms
- "Means" versus "includes" — the critical distinction
- Scope limitations on definition sections

- Act names (short title provisions)

### 4. `numbering-formatting.md`

- SECTION numbering (SECTION 1., SECTION 2.)

- ORS citation format
- Internal cross-references within a bill
- Oregon Constitution citation format
- "To and including" in ORS range citations
- Amendment formatting: bracket deleted text, bold new text
- String citations and "ORS _**to**_" series notation

## Key Principles of Oregon Drafting Style

These principles appear throughout the manual and inform every drafting decision:

1. **Present tense.** Statutes speak continuously. Write "A person who commits..." not "A
   person who shall commit..." or "A person who has committed..."

2. **Active voice.** Assign duties to a named legal subject. "The Director shall adopt rules"
   not "Rules shall be adopted."

3. **Shall, may, must, may not.** These four terms have precise, non-interchangeable meanings.
   "Shall" = mandatory duty. "May" = discretionary permission. "Must" = condition precedent.
   "May not" = prohibition (preferred over "shall not").

4. **Tabular arrangement.** When a sentence contains conditions, exceptions, or lists of three
   or more items, break it into subsections or paragraphs rather than running it on in a single
   sentence.

5. **Consistency.** Use one term for one concept throughout a bill. Never vary phrasing to
   avoid repetition — variation signals a change in meaning.

6. **Single subject.** Each bill must address a single subject under Article IV, section 20, of
   the Oregon Constitution.

7. **No Oxford comma.** Oregon drafting omits the serial comma: "men, women and children"
   not "men, women, and children."

## Limitations and Caveats

- These files capture rules as of the 2024 Edition. Oregon drafting practice may evolve.
- Court decisions cited in the manual interpret Oregon law as of the manual's publication date.
- Administrative and procedural rules specific to the Legislative Assembly (e.g., committee
  procedures, bill introduction deadlines) are not covered here.
- For official drafting work, the full manual and the Legislative Counsel's office are the
  authoritative sources.
