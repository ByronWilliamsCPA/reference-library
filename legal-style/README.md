# Oregon Legal Style Reference

> **Purpose**: Authoritative style and drafting rules for Oregon legislative and court work
> **Sources**: Three official Oregon publications (see below)
> **Last updated**: 2026-02-20

---

## Which Source Governs What

| Document Type | Governing Source |
| --- | --- |
| Court opinions (Court of Appeals, Supreme Court) | Appellate Courts Style Manual |
| Bill drafting by Legislative Counsel | LC Drafting Manual |
| Bill formatting, ORS citations in measures | LC Form & Style Manual |
| ORS citations in court documents | Appellate Courts Style Manual (§ III) |
| Grammar in statutes | LC Drafting Manual (Chapter Three) + LC Form & Style Manual (Chapter 1) |
| Defined terms in bills | LC Drafting Manual (Chapter Seven) |
| Amendment formatting | LC Form & Style Manual (Chapter 6) |

When working with an existing statute or bill (reading or analyzing), the LC sources govern how it was drafted. When writing a court document, the Appellate manual governs. When both could apply, prefer the source specific to your document type.
