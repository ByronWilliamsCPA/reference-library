# Oregon Legislative Citation Rules

**Source**: LC Form and Style Manual for Legislative Measures, 2025-2026 Edition
**Scope**: All citation forms for ORS, Oregon Laws, Oregon Constitution, OAR, ORCP,
current-session measures, federal law, ballot measures, executive orders, and internal references.

---

## Quick Reference

| Citation Type | Basic Form | Example |
|---------------|-----------|---------|
| ORS single section | ORS {chapter}.{section} | ORS 174.100 |
| ORS range | ORS {X} to {Y} | ORS 174.010 to 174.100 |
| ORS series | ORS {X}, {Y} and {Z} | ORS 174.010, 174.020 and 174.030 |
| Oregon Laws chapter | chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year} | chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2021 |
| Oregon Laws section | section {N}, chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year} | section 3, chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2021 |
| Oregon Constitution article | Article {Roman}, Oregon Constitution | Article IV, Oregon Constitution |
| Oregon Constitution section | Article {Roman}, section {N}, Oregon Constitution | Article IV, section 1, Oregon Constitution |
| ORCP | ORCP {number}{letter} | ORCP 21 A |
| OAR | OAR {agency}-{division}-{rule} | OAR 137-003-0501 |
| Current-session bill | {House} Bill {number} ({year}) | House Bill 2001 (2025) |
| Federal statutes | {title} U.S.C. {section} ({year}) | 42 U.S.C. 1983 (2018) |
| C.F.R. | {title} C.F.R. {section} ({year ed.) | 40 C.F.R. 112.1 (2024 ed.) |
| This Act (self-reference) | this {year} Act | this 2025 Act |

## 1. ORS Citations

### 1.1 Basic ORS Citation Form

**Rule**: Cite ORS sections using the chapter number, a period, and the section number. Do not use spaces around the period.

**Examples**:
```
ORS 174.100
ORS 659A.030
ORS 90.100
```

### 1.2 ORS Range: "To" Means "To and Including"

**Rule**: When citing a range of ORS sections, use "to" between the first and last section. "To" means "to and including" — both endpoints and all sections between them are included in the reference.

**Examples**:
```
ORS 174.010 to 174.100
ORS 90.100 to 90.840
```

**Important**: A range citation does not imply that sections are consecutive. Some sections within a range may not exist or may have been repealed.

### 1.3 ORS Series Citations

**Rule**: When citing three or more non-consecutive ORS sections as a series, list them with commas and "and" before the last one. Omit the serial (Oxford) comma before "and."

**Examples**:
```
ORS 174.010, 174.020 and 174.030
ORS 174.010, 174.020, 174.030, 174.040 and 174.050
```

**Rule**: When citing exactly two sections, use "and" without a comma:
```
ORS 174.010 and 174.020
```

### 1.4 String Citations (Mixed Ranges and Single Sections)

**Rule**: When citing a combination of ranges and individual sections, separate them with semicolons. This is sometimes called a "string citation."

**Example**:
```
ORS 174.010 to 174.050; 174.100 and 174.110
```

### 1.5 Citing an ORS Chapter (All Sections)

**Rule**: To refer to an entire ORS chapter, cite it as follows:

**Example**:
```
ORS chapter 174
```

### 1.6 Citing a Series of ORS Chapters

**Example**:
```
ORS chapters 174 and 176
ORS chapters 174, 176 and 178
```

### 1.7 Omitting "ORS" on Repeated Reference

**Rule**: When citing multiple ORS sections in close proximity within a sentence, "ORS" may be omitted after its first appearance if the meaning is clear.

**Example**:
```
ORS 174.010 and 174.020
```
(not "ORS 174.010 and ORS 174.020" unless needed for clarity)

### 1.8 Citing a Subsection or Smaller Unit of an ORS Section

**Rule**: Cite subsections using parenthetical numbers and letters following the section number. No space between the section number and the parenthetical.

**Examples**:
```
ORS 174.100 (1)            [subsection (1)]
ORS 174.100 (1)(a)         [paragraph (a) of subsection (1)]
ORS 174.100 (1)(a)(A)      [subparagraph (A) of paragraph (a) of subsection (1)]
ORS 174.100 (1)(a)(A)(i)   [sub-subparagraph (i)]
```

**Rule**: When citing a subsection or smaller unit, always include the full ORS section number first.

### 1.9 Internal Cross-References Within a Bill

**Rule**: When a bill section refers to another section of the same bill (i.e., a section the bill is creating or amending), use "section {number} of this {year} Act" for new sections or "ORS {number} as amended by section {number} of this {year} Act" for amended sections.

**Examples**:
```
section 3 of this 2025 Act
ORS 174.100 as amended by section 5 of this 2025 Act
```

**Rule**: "This (year) Act" refers to the bill currently being considered. The year used is the session year of that bill, not the current calendar year if different.

**Examples for special sessions**:
```
this 2023 special session Act
this 2025 first special session Act
```

### 1.10 Referring to a Section Being Amended by the Same Bill

**Rule**: A bill section that amends an ORS section creates an awkward situation when that same bill section (or another section of the same bill) needs to refer to the amended ORS section. The correct approach is to cite the ORS section followed by "as amended by section {N} of this {year} Act."

**Example**:
```
ORS 174.100 as amended by section 3 of this 2025 Act
```

**Rule**: When referencing a new (uncodified) section the bill creates, use "section {N} of this {year} Act." After codification, the uncodified reference must be converted to the ORS citation.

### 1.11 Indefinite References to ORS

**Rule**: Avoid indefinite or approximate references to ORS. Cite the specific section. If the exact section is uncertain, use "ORS chapter {N}" for an entire chapter rather than a vague reference.

**Non-standard (avoid)**:
```
under applicable ORS provisions
pursuant to applicable statutes
```

**Standard (preferred)**:
```
ORS 174.100
ORS chapter 174
```

### 1.12 Note Version Citations (Double Amending)

**Rule**: When a bill amends a section that has already been amended in the same session (double amending), the amending clause must cite the "note version" — the ORS section number followed by the note identifying which earlier act amended it.

**Form**: The amending clause reads:
```
SECTION {N}. ORS {section} as amended by section {N} of {earlier bill}, is amended to read:
```

**Example**:
```
SECTION 4. ORS 174.100 as amended by section 2 of this 2025 Act, is amended to read:
```

## 2. Oregon Laws (Session Law) Citations

### 2.1 Basic Oregon Laws Citation Form

**Rule**: Oregon Laws are cited by chapter number and year. Oregon Laws are the session laws — the compilation of all laws enacted during a single legislative session.

**Forms**:
```
chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year}
section {N}, chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year}
```

**Examples**:
```
chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2021
section 3, chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2021
```

### 2.2 Citing Multiple Chapters of Oregon Laws

**Examples**:
```
chapters 5 and 7, Oregon Laws 2021
chapters 5, 7 and 9, Oregon Laws 2021
```

### 2.3 Citing a Range of Sections Within an Oregon Laws Chapter

**Example**:
```
sections 3 to 7, chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2021
```

### 2.4 Citing Session Laws Without ORS Codification

**Rule**: When a session law section has not been codified into ORS (i.e., it is "uncodified session law"), cite it as a section of an Oregon Laws chapter.

**Example**:
```
section 5, chapter 12, Oregon Laws 2023
```

### 2.5 Converting "This (Year) Act" to Oregon Laws Citation

**Rule**: After the session ends and Oregon Laws are published, every internal reference using "this {year} Act" must be converted to the appropriate Oregon Laws chapter citation.

**Before conversion (in draft)**:
```
section 3 of this 2025 Act
```

**After conversion (in Oregon Laws)**:
```
section 3, chapter 47, Oregon Laws 2025
```

### 2.6 Citing Special Session Laws

**Rule**: For laws enacted in special sessions, include the special session designation.

**Examples**:
```
chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2023 (2nd Sp. S.)
section 3, chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2023 (2nd Sp. S.)
```

### 2.7 Amending an Oregon Laws Section

**Rule**: A bill that amends an uncodified Oregon Laws section (session law that was not codified into ORS) uses an amending clause in this form:

```
SECTION {N}. Section {N}, chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year}, is amended to read:
```

**Example**:
```
SECTION 3. Section 5, chapter 12, Oregon Laws 2021, is amended to read:
```

## 3. Oregon Constitution Citations

### 3.1 Citation Form — Statutory Law Referring to the Constitution

**Rule**: When a statute (bill) cites the Oregon Constitution, spell out the full citation with "Oregon Constitution" at the end.

**Form**:
```
Article {Roman numeral}, Oregon Constitution
Article {Roman numeral}, section {N}, Oregon Constitution
Article {Roman numeral}, section {N} ({N}), Oregon Constitution
```

**Examples**:
```
Article IV, Oregon Constitution
Article IV, section 1, Oregon Constitution
Article IV, section 1 (2), Oregon Constitution
Article IV, section 1 (2)(a), Oregon Constitution
```

### 3.2 Citation Form — Internal to the Constitution

**Rule**: Within a joint resolution proposing a constitutional amendment or revision, or within the text of the Oregon Constitution itself, reference other constitutional provisions without appending "Oregon Constitution."

**Examples**:
```
Article IV, section 1
section 1 of this Article
subsection (2) of this section
```

### 3.3 Oregon Constitution Article Names

Some articles are commonly cited with their names:

| Common Name | Citation |
|-------------|----------|
| — | Article I (Bill of Rights) |
| — | Article II (Suffrage and Elections) |
| — | Article III (Distribution of Powers) |
| — | Article IV (Legislative Department) |
| — | Article V (Executive Department) |
| — | Article VI (Administrative Department) |
| — | Article VII (Judicial Department) |
| — | Article VIII (Education and School Funds) |
| — | Article IX (Finance) |
| — | Article X (Militia) |
| — | Article XI (Corporations and Internal Improvements) |
| — | Article XIV (Boundaries) |
| — | Article XV (Miscellaneous) |
| — | Article XVII (Amendments and Revisions) |

**Rule**: Capitalize "Article" and use Roman numerals. Do not abbreviate "Article" as "Art."

### 3.4 Citing an Oregon Constitution Subsection or Paragraph

**Rule**: If a constitutional section has numbered subsections or lettered paragraphs, include them in parentheses following the section number.

**Examples**:
```
Article IV, section 1 (2), Oregon Constitution
Article IV, section 1 (2)(c), Oregon Constitution
```

### 3.5 Ratifying United States Constitutional Amendments

**Rule**: Bills ratifying proposed amendments to the United States Constitution have a distinctive form. The amending clause is:

```
SECTION 1. The proposed amendment to the Constitution of the United States of America
submitted by Congress to the states for ratification as the {ordinal} article of amendment to the
Constitution of the United States is hereby ratified by the Legislative Assembly of the State of
Oregon.
```

### 3.6 Citing United States Constitution

**Rule**: Cite the United States Constitution by article or amendment number.

**Examples**:
```
Article I, United States Constitution
the Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution
```

**Rule**: "United States Constitution" is always spelled out. Do not abbreviate.

## 4. Current-Session Measure Citations

### 4.1 Citing Bills Introduced in the Current Session

**Rule**: Cite a bill introduced in the current session by its chamber, type, and number, followed by the year in parentheses.

**Forms**:
```
House Bill {number} ({year})
Senate Bill {number} ({year})
House Joint Resolution {number} ({year})
Senate Joint Resolution {number} ({year})
House Concurrent Resolution {number} ({year})
Senate Concurrent Resolution {number} ({year})
```

**Examples**:
```
House Bill 2001 (2025)
Senate Bill 100 (2025)
House Joint Resolution 20 (2025)
```

### 4.2 Citing Current-Session Bills in Bill Titles

**Rule**: When a bill title refers to another current-session bill, use the full citation form.

**Example**:
```
Relating to [subject]; creating new provisions; amending House Bill 2001 (2025); ...
```

### 4.3 Blanks for Bills Not Yet Numbered

**Rule**: If a bill is being drafted that will amend a companion bill that has not yet received its number, use a blank line for the number to be filled in later.

**Example**:
```
House Bill ____ (2025)
```

### 4.4 Citing Special Session Bills

**Rule**: Cite measures from a special session by including the special session designation.

**Examples**:
```
House Bill 2 (2023 special session)
Senate Bill 1 (2025 first special session)
```

### 4.5 Citing a Vetoed Bill

**Rule**: Cite a vetoed bill using the bill number and session year. Include context to clarify that it was vetoed, if relevant.

**Example**:
```
House Bill 2001 (2023) (vetoed)
```

### 4.6 Citing an Overturned Veto

**Rule**: When citing a bill whose veto was overturned, cite it like a regular law but note the veto history in context if needed.

## 5. Internal References Within a Measure

### 5.1 Referring to Another Section of the Same Bill

**Rule**: Use "section {N} of this {year} Act" to refer to another section of the same bill.

**Examples**:
```
section 3 of this 2025 Act
sections 3 and 5 of this 2025 Act
sections 3 to 7 of this 2025 Act
```

### 5.2 Referring to a Subsection or Smaller Unit Within the Same Section

**Rule**: Use relative references without citing the full section number.

**Examples**:
```
subsection (2) of this section
paragraph (a) of this subsection
subparagraph (A) of this paragraph
```

### 5.3 Referring to the Same Paragraph or Subsection

**Examples**:
```
this subsection
this paragraph
this subparagraph
```

### 5.4 Referring to Another Subsection in the Same Section

**Examples**:
```
subsection (3) of this section
subsection (3)(a) of this section
```

### 5.5 Referring to Text in a Different Section of the Same ORS Chapter

**Rule**: Use the ORS citation even if the section is in the same chapter.

**Example**:
```
as provided in ORS 174.020
```

### 5.6 Converting Internal References When a Section Is Renumbered

**Rule**: When a bill renumbers subsections or paragraphs within a section being amended, all internal references to those renumbered units must be updated throughout the bill.

## 6. ORCP Citations

### 6.1 Basic ORCP Citation Form

**Rule**: Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure (ORCP) are cited differently from ORS. ORCP uses a rule number followed immediately (no space) by a letter designation.

**Form**:
```
ORCP {number} {letter}
```

**Examples**:
```
ORCP 21 A
ORCP 47 C
ORCP 68 C(4)(a)
```

**Rule**: There is a space between the rule number and the letter. Subsections of an ORCP rule are cited by appending additional designators without additional spaces.

### 6.2 Citing a Full ORCP Rule

**Rule**: When citing an entire ORCP rule (all of its sections), use only the rule number.

**Example**:
```
ORCP 21
```

### 6.3 Citing Multiple ORCP Rules

**Examples**:
```
ORCP 21 A and 21 B
ORCP 21 and 47
```

### 6.4 Internal ORCP Cross-References

**Rule**: Within an ORCP rule itself, cross-references to other rules use the same citation form as external references.

**Example**:
```
As provided in ORCP 21 A
```

## 7. OAR Citations (Oregon Administrative Rules)

### 7.1 Basic OAR Citation Form

**Rule**: Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR) are cited using three-part hyphenated numbers: agency chapter number (three digits), division number (three digits), and rule number (four digits).

**Form**:
```
OAR {agency}-{division}-{rule}
```

**Examples**:
```
OAR 137-003-0501
OAR 291-105-0005
OAR 340-012-0050
```

**Rule**: Use leading zeros to fill all three fields to their required digit lengths (agency: 3 digits, division: 3 digits, rule: 4 digits).

### 7.2 Citing a Range of OAR Rules

**Rule**: When citing a range of OAR rules within the same agency and division, cite the first and last rule numbers.

**Example**:
```
OAR 137-003-0501 to 137-003-0600
```

### 7.3 Citing Multiple OAR Rules

**Example**:
```
OAR 137-003-0501 and 137-003-0600
OAR 137-003-0501, 137-003-0550 and 137-003-0600
```

### 7.4 OAR Numbering Structure

- **Agency chapter**: Three-digit number assigned to each state agency (e.g., 137 = Department of Justice, 291 = Department of Corrections, 340 = Department of Environmental Quality)
- **Division**: Three-digit subdivision within the agency chapter
- **Rule**: Four-digit rule number within the division

## 8. Federal Statute Citations

### 8.1 Citing United States Code

**Rule**: Cite federal statutes using the United States Code (U.S.C.) title and section numbers, followed by the year of the edition in parentheses.

**Form**:
```
{title} U.S.C. {section} ({year})
```

**Examples**:
```
42 U.S.C. 1983 (2018)
29 U.S.C. 201 to 219 (2018)
26 U.S.C. 501(c)(3) (2018)
```

**Rule**: Use the year of the most recent edition of the United States Code that contains the cited provision. This is typically the year of the most recent official edition or supplement.

### 8.2 Citing the Code of Federal Regulations

**Rule**: Cite C.F.R. by title and section number, followed by the year of the edition.

**Form**:
```
{title} C.F.R. {section} ({year} ed.)
```

**Examples**:
```
40 C.F.R. 112.1 (2024 ed.)
29 C.F.R. 1910.132 (2024 ed.)
```

### 8.3 Citing Federal Public Laws

**Rule**: Federal public laws are cited by their public law number and year.

**Form**:
```
Public Law {number}, {year}
P.L. {number} ({year})
```

**Example**:
```
Public Law 93-112, 1973
```

### 8.4 Citing Federal Statutes by Popular Name

**Rule**: Federal statutes with well-known popular names may be cited by name, but the U.S.C. citation should accompany first use.

**Example**:
```
the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (42 U.S.C. 12101 et seq. (2018))
```

## 9. Ballot Measure Citations

### 9.1 Citing Statewide Ballot Measures

**Rule**: Statewide ballot measures are cited by their ballot measure number and election year.

**Form**:
```
Ballot Measure {number} ({year})
```

**Examples**:
```
Ballot Measure 67 (2010)
Ballot Measure 5 (1990)
```

### 9.2 Distinguishing Legislative Measures from Ballot Measures

**Rule**: In documents that refer to both legislative measures (bills) and statewide ballot measures, always use "Ballot Measure {number}" for ballot measures to distinguish them from bills.

**Example**:
```
Use "Ballot Measure 67 (2010)" not "Measure 67" when clarity is needed.
```

### 9.3 Citing Initiative Petitions

**Rule**: Initiative petitions (before they become ballot measures) are cited by their petition number.

**Example**:
```
Initiative Petition 10 (2024)
```

## 10. Executive Order Citations

**Rule**: Oregon executive orders are cited by their number and year.

**Form**:
```
Executive Order {number}-{year}
```

**Example**:
```
Executive Order 21-02
```

## 11. Bill Title Citation Rules

### 11.1 Listing Amended and Repealed ORS Sections in a Bill Title

**Rule**: A bill title must identify ORS sections being amended or repealed. They are listed numerically in the relating clause portion of the title.

**Form**:
```
amending ORS {section};
amending ORS {section} and {section};
repealing ORS {section};
amending ORS {section} and repealing ORS {section};
```

**Example**:
```
Relating to employment; amending ORS 659A.030, 659A.040 and 659A.050; repealing ORS 659A.060; and prescribing an effective date.
```

### 11.2 Listing New ORS Provisions in a Bill Title

**Rule**: When a bill creates new provisions that will be codified into ORS, the title states "creating new provisions of ORS" but does not list specific new section numbers (because they are assigned during codification, after the session).

**Example**:
```
Relating to housing; creating new provisions; amending ORS 456.100 and 456.200.
```

### 11.3 Bill Titles for Measures That Cite an Earlier Bill

**Rule**: When a bill amends a bill from the current session, cite the current-session bill.

**Example**:
```
Relating to education; amending House Bill 2001 (2025).
```

## 12. Measure Summary Citation Rules

### 12.1 ORS Citations Omitted from Measure Summaries

**Rule**: Measure summaries do not include ORS citations. The summary describes what the measure does in plain language without citing specific ORS section numbers.

**Standard**:
```
Correct: "Prohibits an employer from discriminating against an employee based on..."
Avoid:   "Amends ORS 659A.030 to prohibit an employer from..."
```

### 12.2 Oregon Laws Citations in Measure Summaries

**Rule**: Oregon Laws citations may appear in measure summaries if the measure directly amends an uncodified session law and the citation is necessary for the reader to understand what is being changed.

## 13. Amending Clause Citation Forms

### 13.1 Standard Amending Clause for an ORS Section

**Form**:
```
SECTION {N}. ORS {section} is amended to read:
```

**Example**:
```
SECTION 3. ORS 174.100 is amended to read:
```

### 13.2 Amending Clause for Multiple ORS Sections (Separate Sections)

**Rule**: Each ORS section being amended gets its own bill section with its own amending clause. It is not permissible to amend multiple ORS sections within a single bill section.

### 13.3 Amending Clause for an Oregon Laws Section

**Form**:
```
SECTION {N}. Section {N}, chapter {N}, Oregon Laws {year}, is amended to read:
```

**Example**:
```
SECTION 3. Section 5, chapter 12, Oregon Laws 2021, is amended to read:
```

### 13.4 Amending Clause for an ORCP Rule

**Form**:
```
SECTION {N}. ORCP {number} is amended to read:
```

**Rule**: An ORCP rule is amended as a complete unit (the full rule text is set forth). Individual ORCP lettered sections are amended by amending the rule that contains them.

### 13.5 Repealing Clause for ORS

**Form**:
```
SECTION {N}. ORS {section} is repealed.
```

**Rule**: List sections being repealed in numerical order within the repealing clause section.

**Example** (multiple sections):
```
SECTION 5. ORS 174.010, 174.020 and 174.030 are repealed.
```

### 13.6 Adding Clause for New ORS Sections

**Rule**: Adding clauses are used to add new sections to ORS chapters or to existing series. The ORS editor assigns the final ORS numbers after codification.

**Form for adding to a chapter**:
```
SECTION {N}. {New section text is set forth in full using boldfaced type.}
```

**Form for adding a section to an existing series** (after the series's last existing section):
```
SECTION {N}. ORS {series last section} is amended by adding a new section to read:
{New section text in boldfaced type}
```

## 14. Special Citation Situations

### 14.1 Citing Sections of a Bill That Add to a Series

**Rule**: When a bill adds new sections to an existing ORS series, the bill section numbers (SECTION 1, SECTION 2, etc.) are used during the session. Those references are converted to ORS citations when the session laws are codified.

### 14.2 Citing Measures from Past Special Sessions

**Rule**: Measures from past special sessions are cited by their designation, including the special session name.

**Examples**:
```
chapter 5, Oregon Laws 2023 (2nd Sp. S.)
House Bill 2 (2023 second special session)
```

### 14.3 Citing Ballot Measures in Explanatory Statements

**Rule**: In explanatory statements, the first reference to a statewide measure should use the full "Ballot Measure {number}" form. Second and subsequent references may use "measure" or "ballot measure."

**Example**:
```
First reference: "Ballot Measure 10 amends the Oregon Constitution..."
Second reference: "The measure allows the state to hold and dispose of stock..."
```
