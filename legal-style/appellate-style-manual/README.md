# Oregon Appellate Courts Style Manual — Reference Overview

> **Source**: Oregon Appellate Courts Style Manual (Updated 2023)
> **Applies to**: Court opinions issued by the Oregon Court of Appeals, Oregon Supreme Court, and Oregon Tax Court
> **Does NOT apply to**: Briefs, statutes, administrative rules as drafted by agencies, or other legal documents outside appellate opinions
> **Contact for questions not covered**: publications@ojd.state.or.us

## About This Manual

The Oregon Appellate Courts adopted this style manual as a guideline for conventions used in format, citation, quotation, and style when writing opinions. As the preface states, "It is not all-inclusive nor an attempt to dictate writing style." See ORAP 5.20(45) (referring to Style Manual as guide to conventions in style and citation).

## Scope

This manual governs the following opinion types:

- **Signed opinions** — majority, concurring, specially concurring, concurring in part/dissenting in part, and dissenting
- **Per Curiam opinions** — used for summary dispositions and all lawyer discipline, Bar admission, and judicial fitness matters before the Supreme Court; carry same weight of authority as signed opinions
- **Nonprecedential memorandum opinions** (Court of Appeals) — cited only in accordance with ORAP 10.30
- **Affirmed Without Opinion (AWOP)** — title page only; no precedential value
- **Affirmed by an Equally Divided Court** — resolved by opinion, may consist of title page only

## Default Citation Authority

When a citation question is not addressed in this manual, the courts follow **The Bluebook, A Uniform System of Citation** (most current version, published by the Harvard Law Review Association).

## Default Style References

When a style question is not addressed in this manual, the courts consult:

- **Webster's Third New Int'l Dictionary** (unabridged ed 2002) — official dictionary for spelling, word usage, and hyphenation (adopted 1993)
- **The Chicago Manual of Style** (14th ed 1993)
- **Bryan A. Garner, A Dictionary of Modern American Usage** (1998)
- **Bryan A. Garner, A Dictionary of Modern Legal Usage** (2d ed 1995)
- **William A. Sabin, The Gregg Reference Manual** (9th ed 1999)

## Key Baseline Rules

- Citations should be made to **official print sources** whenever possible
- Slip opinions are **double spaced**; indented quoted material is **single spaced**
- Font: **Times New Roman, 13-point**
- Footnotes appear at the **bottom of the page** on which they are referenced
- Line numbers run along the **left-hand margin** (except the footnote section)
- Contractions are used **only when quoting** from a source in which they appear
- The courts generally **do not use periods in citations**
