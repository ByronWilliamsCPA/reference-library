# Citations: Oregon Appellate Courts Style Manual

> **Source**: Oregon Appellate Courts Style Manual (Updated 2023)
> **Applies to**: Court opinions (Oregon Court of Appeals, Oregon Supreme Court, Oregon Tax Court)
> **Contact for questions not covered**: <publications@ojd.state.or.us>

## Quick Reference

**Oregon Supreme Court full citation:**

> PGE v. Bureau of Labor and Industries, 317 Or 606, 859 P2d 1143 (1993).

**Oregon Court of Appeals full citation:**

> Lesch v. DeWitt, 118 Or App 397, 847 P2d 888 (1993).

**Short citation (after first full citation in text):**

> Bates, 304 Or at 522.

**ORS citation (narrative):** ORS 161.155(1)(a)(A) — **do not use et seq.**
**ORS citation form for sequence:** ORS 30.150 - 30.175

**Oregon Constitution citation form:**

> Or Const, Art I, § 17.

**OAR citation:**

> OAR 253-004-0001.

**No periods after abbreviations** (except when quoting material that includes them)

**No "supra"** as substitute for short citations — the Oregon appellate courts do not use supra.

## Section I: Organization and Arrangement

### I.A — The Bluebook

**Rule**: The appellate courts generally follow the citation practices in the most current
version of The Harvard Law Review Association's *The Bluebook, A Uniform System of Citation*,
except as noted in this Style Manual. The Bluebook is the default source for citation questions
not addressed here.

### I.B — Consistency of Citations

**Rule**: Cite authority in a clear, sensible manner that will convey the information needed to
find the cited authority. Consistency within a particular document is important.

**Rule — Same-year subsequent history**: If all decisions in one case take place during the
same year, place the date (year) once at the end of the entire citation. If the decisions span
more than one year, place all years in the appropriate places.

**Standard abbreviations for prior or subsequent case history:**

| Abbreviated Form | Full Term |
|-----------------|-----------|
| adh'd to on recons | adhered to on reconsideration |
| aff'd | affirmed |
| cert | certiorari |
| cert den | certiorari denied |
| recons | reconsideration (not "recon") |
| reh'g | rehearing |
| rem'd | remanded |
| rev'd | reversed |
| rev | revised |
| rev den | review denied |
| vac'd | vacated |
| writ den | writ of mandamus denied |

**Terms not abbreviated:**

allowed (not "all"), appeal dismissed, as improvidently allowed, compiled as a note after,
decision by order, dismissed (not "dism"), modified (not "mod"), overruled on other grounds

### I.C — Case Names

**Rule**: When citing Oregon appellate cases, do NOT use the title page, a regional reporter,
Premise, Westlaw, or LEXIS as the source for the official case name. Use the case name exactly
as published in the official state reporter, located at the top of the odd- or even-numbered
pages. In the Oregon Reports and Oregon Appellate Courts Advance Sheets, the case name appears
on the even-numbered pages.

**Rule — VAWA cases**: Per Joint CJO 23-012/23-01 (effective April 1, 2023), when citing any
VAWA case, modify the case name to include only the initials of the protected party, regardless
of how the case name was previously published. VAWA cases include civil stalking, Family Abuse
Prevention Act, Elderly Persons with Disabilities Abuse Prevention Act, Registration of Foreign
Restraining Orders, Sexual Abuse Protection Orders, Extreme Risk Protection Orders, and
Punitive Contempt Cases.

**Rule — Older bound volumes**: In older bound volumes of the Oregon Reports, case names are
shown with small uppercase letters. Replace those with ordinary Roman type for citation
purposes. When older volumes show *et al.*, *et ux.*, or *et vir.* to indicate additional
parties, include those abbreviations and their punctuation for consistency.

**Rule — Jurisdiction without own reporter**: If citing a case from a jurisdiction that does
not have its own separate official reporter or if you do not have access to the official
reports, use the case name as used in the regional reporter or follow the naming conventions
in The Bluebook when using online services.

### I.D — Spaces and Abbreviations

**Rule**: Spaces in citations are used to separate longer abbreviations.

**Examples**:

> Correct: S Ct, F Supp, L Ed 2d, Or L Rev, Tex App (spaces between abbreviations of two or
> more letters)
> Correct: P3d, NE2d, NYS2d (no space between adjacent single uppercase letters or numerals
> and ordinals treated as single uppercase letters)
> Correct: So 2d (space before abbreviation containing two or more letters)

**Rule**: Periods are not used after abbreviations, except when quoting material in which they
are included.

### I.E — String Citations

**Rule**: When using string citations, follow The Bluebook format for the order of authorities:

1. Cases decided by the same court, or by all federal circuit courts of appeals or federal
   district courts, are arranged in **reverse chronological order**
2. Different courts are set out **by rank**

**Rule**: Use a **semicolon**, not a comma or the word "and," to separate citations within a
string.

### I.F — Signals

**Rule**: Introductory signals are italicized. "See" is not italicized when it functions as a
verb (not a signal).

**Examples**:

> Correct (signal): *See*, e.g., OEC 401 (regarding relevancy of evidence).
> Correct (verb): For discussion on the merits, see Smith, 300 Or App at 150.

**Signal meanings:**

| Signal | Use |
|--------|-----|
| *see*, *see also*, *accord*, *cf.* | Support |
| *compare* ... *and* / *compare* ... *with* | Comparison |
| *contra*, *but see*, *but cf.* | Contradiction |
| *see generally* | Background |

**Rule**: Like signals are grouped into a single citation sentence, separated by a semicolon.

**Rule**: No signal is needed when the cited authority directly states the proposition. When
using signals, the courts typically include a parenthetical explanation briefly describing the
relevance of the authority cited.

**Rule**: The internal comma within a signal is italicized (e.g., *See, e.g.,*).

**Example**:

> Cf. State v. Brown, 300 Or 125, 130, 860 P2d 498 (1985) (hearsay inadmissible at trial).

### I.G — Parenthetical Information

**Rule**: Parenthetical explanations are added as needed to describe the relevance of a cited
authority. When including a parenthetical explanation in a case with citations to subsequent
history, place the explanation **following all the subsequent case history**.

**Rule**: Quoted statements that read as a complete sentence should include an initial uppercase
letter and period (with brackets and/or an ellipsis, if appropriate). Other material that reads
as a sentence does not receive an initial uppercase letter or a period.

**Examples**:

> Old v. Navy, 555 Or App 444, 447, 222 P3d 888 (2015) ("[An] award of 'reasonable' attorney
> fees does not preclude the use of a multiplier or other fee enhancement \* \* \*. Such an
> enhancement may be applied at the beginning of the calculation process.").
>
> Aber v. Crombie, 123 Or 234, 236, 456 P3d 678 (2015) (the constitution does not provide
> that shirts have to be "button down").

**Rule**: When appending information to a citation using terms such as *cited in*, *quoted in*,
*construed in*, those words are italicized.

**Example**:

> Keenan v. Norris-Lampe, 330 Or 456, 777 P2d 897 (1999), *construed in* Bennett v. Bauman,
> 333 Or 566, 790 P2d 654 (2001).

**Rule**: When including works that the primary authority quotes, discusses, or mentions, the
lead-in words should be indicated parenthetically and not italicized.

**Example**:

> See Keenan v. Norris-Lampe, 330 Or 456, 777 P2d 897 (1999) (citing Bennett v. Bauman,
> 329 Or 566, 770 P2d 654 (1998)).

## Section II: Case Law

### II.A — Oregon Full Citations

**Rule**: When citing a case for the first time in the text of an opinion (majority, concurring,
or dissenting), use the full case citation. If the case has previously been cited in full in a
footnote, the first textual citation must still be a full citation.

**Full citation format**: \[case name\] \[volume\] \[reporter\] \[beginning page\],
\[pinpoint page\], \[parallel reporter citation\] \[year\]\[subsequent history\]\[parenthetical\]

#### Oregon Supreme Court

**Rule**: Oregon Supreme Court cases issued before 1888 have no parallel citations.

**Basic form**:

> PGE v. Bureau of Labor and Industries, 317 Or 606, 859 P2d 1143 (1993).

**On rehearing — citing the initial decision**:

> State v. Laundy, 103 Or 443, 455, 204 P 958 (1921), reh'g den, 103 Or 503, 206 P 290 (1922).

**On rehearing — citing the rehearing decision**:

> State v. Laundy, 103 Or 443, 503, 206 P 290 (1922) (on rehearing).

**On reconsideration (specify disposition)**:

> Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. v. Tualatin Tire & Auto, 322 Or 406, 908 P2d 300 (1995),
> modified on recons, 325 Or 46, 932 P2d 1141 (1997).

**Overruled by subsequent Oregon case**:

> Rose v. Port of Portland, 82 Or 541, 552, 162 P 498 (1917), overruled in part on other
> grounds by State ex rel Heinig v. Milwaukie et al, 231 Or 473, 479, 373 P2d 680 (1962).

**Rejected by subsequent Oregon case**:

> State v. Smith, 295 Or 200, 625 P2d 20 (1981), rejected in part by State v. Jones, 321 Or
> 100, 805 P2d 150 (1991).

**Rule**: Other alternatives, such as "abrogated by" and "questioned by," can be used as
appropriate.

**Certiorari denied by United States Supreme Court**:

> Dept. of Trans. v. Lundberg, 312 Or 568, 825 P2d 641, cert den, 506 US 975 (1992).
> Whitman v. United States, 904 F Supp 2d 363 (SDNY 2012), writ den, 574 US \_\_\_, 135 S Ct
> 352 (2014).

**Rule**: Always include cert den, if applicable. If certiorari is dismissed, substitute "cert
dismissed" for "cert den." Unless certiorari is denied or dismissed by opinion, parallel
citations are not necessary for "cert den" or "cert dismissed" after they are published in the
United States Reports.

**Certiorari granted (include as much information as available)**:

> State v. Ice, 343 Or 248, 70 P3d 1049, cert granted, \_\_ US \_\_, 128 S Ct 1657 (2008).
> State v. Ice, 343 Or 248, 70 P3d 1049, cert granted, 552 US 1256 (2008).

**Supreme Court opinion reversed by United States Supreme Court**:

> Gilliam County v. Dept. of Environmental Quality, 316 Or 99, 849 P2d 500 (1993), rev'd and
> rem'd sub nom Oregon Waste Systems, Inc. v. Department of Environmental Quality of Ore.,
> 511 US 93, 114 S Ct 1345, 128 L Ed 2d 13 (1994).

**Rule — Sub nom**: When the name of the case differs in prior or subsequent history, the new
name must be given, except (1) when the parties' names merely are reversed; (2) when the
citation in which the difference occurs is to a denial of certiorari or rehearing; or (3) when,
in the appeal of an administrative action, the name of the private party remains the same.

**Rule — Use of "by" vs. *sub nom***: Use the word "by" when referring to subsequent case
history only if referring to an entirely different case. Otherwise, use *sub nom*.

> Correct: Keenan v. Norris-Lampe, 330 Or 456, 777 P2d 897 (1999), overruled on other grounds
> by Bennett v. Bauman, 333 Or 566, 790 P2d 654 (2001).
> Correct: Smith v. Jones, 330 Or 456, 777 P2d 897 (1999), rev'd on other grounds sub nom
> Jones and White v. Smith, 668 US 123, 113 S Ct 1346, 129 L Ed 2d 14 (2001).

#### Oregon Court of Appeals

**Precedential opinions**:

> Lesch v. DeWitt, 118 Or App 397, 847 P2d 888 (1993).

**Nonprecedential memorandum opinions** (cited in accordance with ORAP 10.30):

> State v. Nord, 320 Or App 672 (2022) (nonprecedential memorandum opinion).

**Rule**: The parenthetical for nonprecedential memorandum opinions concerns weight of authority
and therefore precedes any subsequent history.

**Example**:

> Party v. Party, 320 Or App 486 (nonprecedential memorandum opinion), rev den, 370 Or 56
> (2022).

**Overruled by subsequent Oregon case**:

> Eklund v. Clackamas County, 36 Or App 73, 583 P2d 567 (1978), overruled on other grounds by
> Forman v. Clatsop County, 63 Or App 617, 665 P2d 365 (1983).

**Supreme Court review denied**:

> Allred v. Board of Parole, 124 Or App 278, 862 P2d 546 (1993), rev den, 318 Or 325 (1994).

**More than one petition for review denied**:

> Allred v. Board of Parole, 124 Or App 278, 862 P2d 546 (1993), rev den, 318 Or 325; 318 Or
> 502 (1994).

**Rule**: Always include rev den and cert den, if applicable. Unless the review denied or
certiorari denied citation is not available or there is an opinion denying review or certiorari,
parallel citations are not necessary. Before 1976, disposition of petitions for review were not
published; accordingly, only the year and disposition are indicated: e.g., State v. Smith,
14 Or App 72 (1973), rev den (1974). The current practice of the appellate courts is NOT to
show that review is pending.

**Supreme Court review allowed, but case not yet decided**:

> Lesch v. DeWitt, 118 Or App 397, 847 P2d 888, rev allowed, 317 Or 162 (1993).

**Supreme Court review allowed, then later dismissed**:

> Finch v. Andrews, 124 Or App 558, 863 P2d 496, rev dismissed, 320 Or 267 (1994).

**Rule**: If applicable, it is optional to include "as improvidently allowed" after "rev
dismissed."

**Court of Appeals decision affirmed by Supreme Court**:

> State v. McCoy, 17 Or App 155, 521 P2d 1074, aff'd, 270 Or 340, 527 P2d 725 (1974).

**Court of Appeals decision reversed by Supreme Court**:

> State v. Cloutier, 33 Or App 121, 575 P2d 996, rev'd on other grounds, 286 Or 579, 596 P2d
> 278 (1979).

**Court of Appeals decision remanded by Supreme Court**:

> State v. White, 59 Or App 61, 650 P2d 184 (1982), rem'd, 297 Or 302, 685 P2d 983 (1984).

**Court of Appeals decision on reconsideration (specify disposition)**:

> Kirpal Light Satsang v. Douglas County, 96 Or App 207, 772 P2d 944, adh'd to on recons,
> 97 Or App 614, 776 P2d 1312 (1989).

**Court of Appeals decision vacated by unpublished order**:

> Davis v. Johnson, 155 Or App 266, 958 P2d 907 (1998), decision vac'd by order, July 21,
> 1998.

**Oregon review denied and United States Supreme Court certiorari denied (but not by
opinion)**:

> State Highway Com. v. DeLong Corp., 9 Or App 550, 495 P2d 1215, rev den (1972), cert den,
> 411 US 965 (1973).

**Certiorari denied by opinion by United States Supreme Court**:

> State v. Smith, 30 Or App 462, 575 P2d 369, rev den, 282 Or 823 (1978), cert den, 454 US
> 324, 99 S Ct 379, 25 L Ed 2d 889 (1979).

**Appeal dismissed by United States Supreme Court (by opinion — include parallel citations)**:

> Boykin v. Ott, 10 Or App 210, 498 P2d 815, rev den (1972), appeal dismissed, 411 US 912,
> 36 S Ct 304, 93 L Ed 2d 1554 (1973).

#### Oregon Tax Court

**Regular Division**:

> Fellows v. Dept. of Rev., 14 OTR 13 (1999).

**Unpublished Regular Division Decisions**:

> Fellows v. Dept. of Rev., TC 4952, WL 2037643 (Or Tax, May 24, 1999). (optional to include
> online reference, but recommended)

**Magistrate Division**:

> Jacobs v. Harney Co., 16 OTR-MD 344 (2001).

**Unpublished Magistrate Division Decisions**:

> Jacobs v. Harney Co., TC-MD 994356Z, WL 842084 at \*3 (Or Tax M Div, Apr 7, 2000).
> (optional to include online reference, but recommended)

#### Case Not Yet Appearing in Publication

> Jones v. State of Oregon, \_\_\_ Or \_\_\_, \_\_\_, \_\_\_ P3d \_\_\_ (Apr 1, 2013)
> (slip op at 15:9-16).
> Smith, \_\_\_ Or at \_\_\_ (slip op at 28:6 - 29:2).
> Smith, \_\_\_ Or at \_\_\_, \_\_\_ (slip op at 28:6 - 29:2; slip op at 31:17 - 32:2).
> Smith v. Jones, \_\_\_ Or App \_\_\_, \_\_\_ n 3, \_\_\_ P3d \_\_\_ (Feb 1, 2013)
> (emphasis added) (slip op at 5 n 3).

**Rule**: To ensure correct cross-referencing within a slip opinion, cite the appropriate page
number(s). For added accuracy, the Supreme Court also includes the specific line number(s).

#### Circuit Court Cases

> State v. Cazares-Mendoza, Case No. C062326CR.

#### VAWA Cases

**Case title in court records**: Protected Party v. Second Party
**Title Page format**: P. P. v. SECOND PARTY

**Full citation format (even if previously published as Party v. Party)**:

> P. P. v. Party, 325 Or App 123, 456 P3d 789 (2023).

**Short citation format**:

> P. P., 325 Or App at 124.

**Rule — Redacting to initials**: Use the first letter of each name in the protected party's
name, maintaining hyphens if used.

**Example**: Jane Doe-Smith v. John Doe would be:

- J. D.-S. v. Doe (if Jane is the protected party)
- Doe-Smith v. J. D. (if John is the protected party)

**Rule — Quoting prior decisions using unredacted VAWA case titles**: Use brackets to redact
the name of the protected party to initials.

**Example**:

> " 'Danger' in this context means 'a threat of physical injury, not merely a threat of
> annoyance or harassment.' [K. R.] v. Erazo, 248 Or App 700, 706-07, 274 P3d 214 (2012)."

### II.B — Oregon Short Citations and Other Issues

#### II.B.1 — In General

**Rule**: When citing a case that has already been cited in the text of an opinion, use a short
citation. If the only previous citation to a case is in a footnote, use the full citation the
first time the case is cited in the text.

**Rule**: The shortened case name in a short citation is the **first nongovernmental party**
appearing in the official case name citation.

**Full citation (first reference)**:

> State v. Bates, 304 Or 519, 747 P2d 991 (1987).

**Short citation form — \[shortened case name\], \[volume\] \[reporter\] at \[page\]**:

> Bates, 304 Or at 522. (eliminating the parallel citation)

**Rule**: When citing a specific page and the case name is used in the sentence being cited, or
if there is no doubt as to the case being cited, the case name may be omitted from the
citation.

**Examples**:

> In Bates, this court stated that the officers violated defendant's constitutional rights by
> instructing him to move bag into view. 304 Or at 527.
>
> This court held that the officers violated defendant's constitutional rights by instructing
> him to move the bag into view. Bates, 304 Or at 527.

**Rule**: The appellate courts do not use "supra" as a substitute for short citations.

**Rule**: Even when a governmental official is individually named, use the first nongovernmental
party for the shortened case name.

#### II.B.2 — Variations on Case Name

**Rule**: If a shortened case name causes confusion (e.g., two cases with similar names), use
the full case name for each throughout the opinion.

**Rule**: When both parties have the same last name (as in some domestic relations matters),
use that shared name as the short cite.

> Smith and Smith → short cite: Smith

**Rule — Related cases in a series**: Add a modified case name in parentheses for clarity.

**Example**:

> In re Wyllie, 326 Or 447, 952 P2d 550 (1998) (Wyllie I) (one-year suspension); In re
> Wyllie, 327 Or 175, 957 P2d 1222 (1998) (Wyllie II) (two-year suspension). In Wyllie I,
> \* \* \*

**Rule — Lawyer disciplinary cases**: The shortened case name is the lawyer's last name,
without the *In re* designation.

> In re Jones, 326 Or 195, 951 P2d 149 (1997). (full citation)
> Jones, 326 Or at 198. (short citation)

**Rule — Mandamus cases**: For cases initiated after 1997, the case name is the same as the
proceeding in the lower court, and the judge's name does not appear in the title. In older
mandamus cases, cite as follows:

> State ex rel Huddleston v. Sawyer, 324 Or 597, 932 P2d 1145 (1997). (full)
> Huddleston, 324 Or at 600. (short)

#### II.B.3 — Citation to Series of Pages

**Rule**: When citing a series of pages, indicate as follows (do not truncate in a way that
creates ambiguity):

> Stranahan, 331 Or at 57-58.
> Fugate, 332 Or at 202-03. (not 202-3)
> Joslin, 332 Or at 499-501. (not 499-01)

#### II.B.4 — Use of "id." with Case Names

**Rule**: When citing the immediately preceding authority, use *id.*

> Id. at 525.

**Rule**: If citing the same page of the immediately preceding authority, use *id.* without
the page number.

**Rule**: Do not use *id.* where there is an intervening citation to another authority (be it
a case name, statute, or some other authority). When there is an intervening textual reference,
use *id.* only when there is no danger of ambiguity.

#### II.B.5 — Citation to Footnote

**Full citation**:

> State v. Trenary, 114 Or App 608, 610 n 2, 836 P2d 739 (1992).

**Short citation**:

> Trenary, 114 Or App at 610 n 2.

**Series of footnotes**:

> State v. Trenary, 114 Or App 608, 610 nn 3-5, 836 P2d 739 (1992).
> State v. Trenary, 114 Or App 608, 610 n 3, 611 nn 4 & 5, 836 P2d 739 (1992).

**Rule**: Citation is to the first page on which the footnote appears, even if it spans more
than one page, no matter where the pertinent text actually appears.

**When citing both body text and a footnote on the same page**:

> Trenary, 114 Or App at 610, 610 n 2.
> or: Trenary, 114 Or App at 610 & n 2.

#### II.B.6 — Internal Citations; Dissents; Concurrences

**Rule — Majority citing a dissent or concurrence** (all references, regardless of number):

> The dissent contends that the statute operates prospectively only. \_\_\_ Or App at \_\_\_
> (Smith, J., dissenting) (slip op at 3).

**Rule — Dissent or concurrence citing the majority** (do not include the case name):

> The majority concludes that the statute operates retroactively. \_\_\_ Or App at \_\_\_
> (slip op at 7).

**Rule — Dissent or concurrence citing itself**:

> As noted above, I dissent because I disagree with the majority's statutory construction and
> conclusion. \_\_\_ Or App at \_\_\_ (Smith, J., dissenting) (slip op at 1).

**Rule**: Do not use *supra* or *infra* and do not use only "See note 6" when referring to
earlier or later parts of an opinion; instead, use the short citation with the slip op
reference.

#### II.B.7 — Short Citation Form for Oregon Tax Cases

> Fellows, 14 OTR at 17. (Regular Division)
> Jacobs, 16 OTR-MD at 347. (Magistrate Division)

#### II.B.8 — Possessive Endings on Case Names

**Rule**: When a case name takes a possessive ending, the "'s" is not italicized.

> Correct: Bates's rule applies in this case.

### II.C — Federal Jurisdictions

**Rule**: For United States Supreme Court cases, the case name for citation purposes is taken
from the United States Reports (US), if available (odd-numbered pages); otherwise, use the
case name from an alternative reporter. The short citation is also to the US Reports, if
available; otherwise, cite the Supreme Court reports; if not, then the Lawyer's Edition.

**Rule**: If the US Reports citation is not available, insert "\_\_\_ US \_\_\_" (including a
blank for a jump/pinpoint citation, if applicable) in all full and short citations, before the
citation to an alternative reporter.

#### United States Supreme Court

**First reference**:

> Wagner v. Oregon, 492 US 914, 109 S Ct 3235, 163 L Ed 2d 583 (1989).

**Short citation**:

> Wagner, 492 US at 916.

**Rule**: It is acceptable to refer to *Miranda v. Arizona* without including a full citation,
e.g., "Miranda rights" or "Miranda warnings."

#### Federal Circuit Courts (3d and 2d series)

> Johnson v. Clifton, 74 F3d 1087 (11th Cir), cert den, 519 US 808 (1996).
> Freije v. United States, 408 F2d 100, 102 (1st Cir), cert den, 396 US 859 (1969).
> United States v. Wainwright, 413 F2d 796, 803 (10th Cir 1969), cert den, 396 US 1009 (1970).

#### Federal Supplement

> Lucas v. Seagrave Corporation, 277 F Supp 338 (D Minn 1967).
> United States v. Zeiger, 350 F Supp 685 (DDC), rev'd, 475 F2d 1280 (DC Cir 1972).

**Unpublished federal district court decision**:

> Smith v. Jones, No CV 96-6109-TC (D Or Feb 5, 2001).

**Rule**: For abbreviations of federal district courts, see The Bluebook.

#### Federal Cases with Incomplete Information

#### Federal Tax Cases

**Rule**: Order of authority: (1) United States Supreme Court; (2) federal courts of appeals;

**United States Tax Court**:

### II.D — States Other Than Oregon

**Rule**: For states other than Oregon, cite the official reporter first, then add the regional
reporter when using jump/pinpoint citations (because that benefits readers who have access to
only regional reporters).

**Example**:

> In Statser v. Statser, 205 Okla 608, 611, 239 P2d 764, 766 (1951), the court stated: ...
> Id. at 615, 239 P2d at 768.

**Rule**: For cases not yet appearing in publication, use a citation to Westlaw.

> State v. Smith, No 26245-2-II, 2013 WL 651868 at \*2 (Wash App Div 2, Apr 19, 2013).

**Rule**: When the official reporter (state reporter) is the same for both the highest court
and the intermediate appellate court, include the court abbreviation in the date parenthetical.

> State v. Gray, 231 Ariz 374, 295 P3d 951 (Ariz Ct App 2013).

**Rule**: When the official reporter is the regional reporter, include the court abbreviation
in the date parenthetical.

> Golphin v. State, 945 So 2d 1174 (Fla 2006).

**Rule**: For abbreviations of state and regional reporters, see The Bluebook.

---

### II.E — Online Sources

**Rule**: A case unreported in a print source can be cited if it resides online in a readily
recognized source. Include the case name, number, database identifier, court name, and complete
date, along with any other identifiers pertinent to its location.

> Silva v. Mt. Bachelor, Inc., No CV 06-6330-AA, 2008 WL 2889656 at \*2 (D Or July 21, 2008).

**For cases having a state citation in part and an online citation in part**:

> Zollner v. Smith, 268 Ga App 480, 484, 602 SE2d 140, 141, rev den, 2004 Ga LEXIS 1059
> (2004).

## Section III: Constitutional, Statutory, and Other Related Citations

**Rule**: *Id.* (or *id.* § \_\_\_, if applicable) can be used as a subsequent reference
citation to constitutional provisions, statutory and code provisions (both current provisions
and older codes), legislative commentary, and other types of sources discussed in this section.

### III.A — Oregon Citations

#### III.A.1 — Oregon Constitution

**Narrative form (first reference)**:

> Article I, section 17, of the Oregon Constitution provides that \* \* \*.

**Subsequent reference (narrative)**:

> Article VII (Amended), section 1, provides that \* \* \*.

**Citation form**:

> Or Const, Art I, § 17.
> Or Const, Art VII (Amended), § 3.
> Or Const, Art VII (Original).
> Or Const, Art XI, § 11b(2)(b).
> Or Const, Art I, § 11 (1857). (denoting the original version, NOT the current version)

**Rules**:

- "Article" always begins with an uppercase "A"
- The article number is always a Roman numeral
- "Amended" begins with an uppercase "A"
- In narrative, the word "section" is always lowercase except in a heading or subheading
- Do not use periods after abbreviations
- Original Article VII was replaced in 1910 (hence "Amended")

#### III.A.2 — Oregon Laws (Session Laws)

**Narrative form**:

> Oregon Laws 1989, chapter 790, section 87.
> Oregon Laws 1990, chapter 2, sections 45 to 47 (Special Session).
> Oregon Laws 1995, chapter 790, section 84, directs the commission to \* \* \*.

**Citation form**:

> Or Laws 1989, ch 790, § 87.
> Or Laws 1990, ch 2, §§ 3-10 (Spec Sess).

#### III.A.5 — ORS (Oregon Revised Statutes)

**Rule**: Always use the abbreviated form (ORS) in both narrative references and citations.

**Structure**: ORS 161.155(1)(a)(A) = chapter 161, section 155, subsection (1), paragraph (a),
subparagraph (A)

**Rule**: Numbered paragraphs within a section are referred to as **subsections**; lettered
paragraphs are **paragraphs**. For example, ORS 305.220(1)(a) would be referred to
individually as "paragraph (a) of subsection (1)"; when referring to (1)(a) collectively, use
"paragraph (1)(a)."

**Narrative form examples**:

> ORS chapter 554. (entire chapter)
> ORS 30.150 to 30.175; ORS 250.035(2)(a) to (c). (statutory sequence)
> ORS 30.866 and ORS 163.730. (two statutes)
> ORS 250.035(2)(a) and (b). (two parts of the same statute)

**Citation form examples**:

> ORS ch 554. (entire chapter)
> ORS 30.150 - 30.175; ORS 250.035(2)(a) - (c). (statutory sequence)
> ORS 30.866; ORS 163.730. (two statutes)
> ORS 250.035(2)(a), (b). (two parts of same statute)

**Rule**: When citing a sequence of statutes in citation form, do not use "et seq."; instead,
set out the first and last statutes of the sequence.

**Repealed or renumbered statute examples**:

**Rule**: The word *former* is set in italics. Only a repealed or renumbered statute — not an
amended statute — is referred to as "former."

> *Former* ORS 19.023(2)(a) (1995), renumbered as ORS 19.205(2)(a) (1997). (first reference;
> "1995" refers to year of statute in effect when facts occurred; "1997" refers to year
> renumbered)
> *Former* ORS 19.023(2)(a) (1995). (subsequent reference)
> *Former* ORS 736.317 (1961), repealed by Or Laws 1967, ch 482, § 1. (first reference)
> *Former* ORS 736.317 (1961). (subsequent reference)

**Rule — Amended statutes**: An amended statute is not referred to as "former." When citing an
amended statute, the author may: (1) cite the version in effect at the time of the events in
the case; or (2) cite the current version, if the amendment does not affect the issue. If
choosing (1), include subsequent history on the first reference and the year of the version on
subsequent references. If choosing (2), include a footnote on the first reference explaining
that the current version is used because the amendment does not affect the analysis.

**Amended statute examples**:

> ORS 308.370 - 308.375 (1987), amended by Or Laws 1991, ch 459, §§ 117-120. (citation, first
> reference)
> ORS 308.370 - 308.375 (1987). (citation, subsequent reference)

**Rule**: If a statute has been amended or renumbered more than once, the best practice is to
include the full subsequent history on the first reference or at least in a footnote.

## Section IV: Periodical Articles, Books, Treatises, Restatements

### IV — In General

**Rule**: Cite the author's or editor's full name as found on the title page. For books
compiled and edited by a named editor, place the editor's name where the author's name would
normally be, followed by "ed." (including the period).

**Rule**: Cite the author's first name first. If there are up to three authors, list them all.
If four or more authors are listed, list the first author and use "et al."

**Rule**: For articles written by students, use the author's name (if available), along with,
e.g., "Note," "Comment," or "Case Note."

**Rule**: When citing multivolume sets, place the volume number before the title, not before
the author's name.

**Rule**: Use **italics**, not quotation marks, to indicate the name of a book, article, or
treatise.

**Rule**: "Ed." (with a period) refers to "editor"; "ed" (without a period) refers to
"edition."

### IV.A — Periodical Articles

**Full citation format**: \[Author\], \[Name of article (in italics)\], \[volume number\]
\[periodical\] \[first page\], \[cited page(s)\] \[date\]

> Pamela S. Karlan, *Contingent Fees and Criminal Cases*, 93 Colum L Rev 595, 602-03 (1993).
> Laurence H. Tribe & Michael C. Dorf, *Levels of Generality in the Definition of Rights*,
> 57 U Chi L Rev 1057 (1990).
> John Sedgwick, *The Complexity Problem*, The Atlantic 96 (Mar 1993). (newspaper/popular
> periodical: omit volume and issue numbers)

**Short citation form — \[Author's last name\], \[Volume\] \[Periodical\] at \[page\]**:

> Karlan, 93 Colum L Rev at 604.

**Rule**: To determine the proper abbreviation for periodicals, first check the periodical
itself. If it provides no citation form, follow The Bluebook abbreviations.

**Rule**: Omit volume and issue numbers for newspapers and popular periodicals, because the
numbers might not be easily found.

### IV.B — Books and Treatises

**Full citation format**: \[Author(s)\], \[Volume number (if applicable)\] \[Title (in
italics)\] \[subdivision, chapter or section (if applicable)\], \[page(s)\] (\[Edition (if
there is more than one edition)\] \[Year\])

> Wayne R. LaFave, 3 *Search and Seizure* § 7.1(c), 17 (3d ed 1996).
> Bryan A. Garner, *A Dictionary of Modern Legal Usage* 138-39 (1987).
> Laird C. Kirkpatrick, *Oregon Evidence* § 401.02, Art IV-4 (4th ed 2002).
> Webster's Third New Int'l Dictionary 930 (unabridged ed 2002).
> Black's Law Dictionary 700 (7th ed 1999).
> Thomas W. Lippman ed., *The Washington Post Deskbook on Style* 49-55 (2d ed 1989).
> William M. Collier, 3 *Collier on Bankruptcy* 506.04[1] (Lawrence P. King ed., 15th ed
> 1989).
> Jacob Mertens, Jr., 8 *Mertens Law of Federal Income Taxation* § 32B:01 (rev 1999).
> Jacob Mertens, Jr., 15 *Mertens Law of Federal Income Taxation* § 56:45 (Supp 2002).
> (citing supplement)
> Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders 478 (5th ed 2013) (DSM-5). (first
> reference; include parenthetical if subsequent references follow)
> Paul Finkleman & Martin J. Hershock eds., *The History of Michigan Law* 169 (2006),
> available at [URL] (accessed Mar 24, 2015).

**Rule**: It is also acceptable to cite a source with multiple authors using just the first
author's last name followed by *et al* where saving space is desired:

> Henifin et al, *Reference Guide on Medical Testimony*, in *Reference Manual on Scientific
> Evidence* 439, 481 (Federal Judicial Center ed., 2d ed 2000).

**Short citation form**: \[Author's last name\], \[Volume number (if applicable)\] \[Title
(shortened if necessary)\] \[subdivision (if applicable)\] at \[page(s)\]

> LaFave, 3 *Search and Seizure* § 7.1 at 356.
> Garner, *Modern Legal Usage* at 378-79.
> Webster's at 1935.
> DSM-5 at 480.
